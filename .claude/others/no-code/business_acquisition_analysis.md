# **Complete LLM Instructions for Business Acquisition Analysis**

## **INSTRUCTION SET FOR COMPREHENSIVE BUSINESS EVALUATION**

### **ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW**

You are to perform a thorough business acquisition analysis. Follow these instructions systematically, addressing each section completely. Use the provided business information to create a detailed, data-driven evaluation that will guide the acquisition decision.

------

## **SECTION 1: FINANCIAL ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS**

### **1.1 Historical Performance Evaluation**

- Extract all revenue figures from the provided financial statements for each available year
- Calculate year-over-year growth rates for both revenue and SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings)
- Identify and explain any significant jumps or declines in performance (changes over 20%)
- Create a trend analysis showing the trajectory of the business over the past 3-5 years
- Calculate the compound annual growth rate (CAGR) for revenue and SDE
- Assess whether current year projections are based on full-year or partial-year data, and adjust accordingly

### **1.2 Profitability Analysis**

- Calculate gross profit margins for each year and identify trends
- Calculate SDE margins (SDE/Revenue) for each year
- Compare operating expense ratios year-over-year
- Identify the largest expense categories and their proportion to revenue
- Analyze cost of goods sold (if applicable) and its relationship to revenue
- Evaluate EBITDA if different from SDE

### **1.3 Revenue Stream Decomposition**

- Break down revenue by each major source (products, services, recurring, one-time)
- Calculate the percentage contribution of each revenue stream
- Assess the stability and predictability of each revenue source
- Identify any concentration risks in revenue streams
- Evaluate seasonal patterns in revenue generation
- Analyze customer concentration (if data available)

### **1.4 Add-backs and Adjustments Verification**

- List all owner add-backs claimed in the SDE calculation
- Evaluate the legitimacy of each add-back (personal expenses, one-time costs, owner compensation)
- Identify any questionable or aggressive add-backs
- Calculate adjusted SDE if certain add-backs appear invalid
- Note any missing expenses that should be deducted from SDE

------

## **SECTION 2: VALUATION ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS**

### **2.1 Multiple Analysis**

- Calculate the asking price as a multiple of SDE (Price/SDE)
- Calculate the asking price as a multiple of revenue (Price/Revenue)
- Calculate the asking price as a multiple of EBITDA if available
- Research and state typical multiples for this industry and business size
- Compare the asking multiple to industry benchmarks
- Determine if the multiple is justified based on business characteristics

### **2.2 Asset-Based Valuation**

- List all tangible assets included in the sale (equipment, inventory, improvements)
- Assign estimated market values to each asset category
- Calculate total tangible asset value
- Identify any intangible assets (licenses, permits, intellectual property)
- Value intangible assets if possible
- Calculate goodwill (Purchase Price minus Total Asset Value)

### **2.3 Cash Flow Return Analysis**

- Calculate the expected annual return on investment (SDE/Purchase Price)
- Determine the payback period without considering financing
- Calculate ROI under different growth scenarios (0%, 5%, 10% growth)
- Assess working capital requirements
- Determine additional investment needs post-purchase

### **2.4 Financing Impact Assessment**

- Calculate monthly debt service based on typical SBA loan terms (if mentioned)
- Subtract debt service from SDE to determine actual owner benefit
- Calculate debt service coverage ratio (DSCR)
- Assess the impact of any seller financing on cash flow
- Determine minimum down payment required
- Calculate break-even point including debt service

------

## **SECTION 3: OPERATIONAL ASSESSMENT INSTRUCTIONS**

### **3.1 Business Model Analysis**

- Describe the core business model in detail
- Identify key operational processes
- Assess scalability of the current model
- Identify operational bottlenecks or constraints
- Evaluate the complexity of operations
- Determine ease of replication or expansion

### **3.2 Customer Base Evaluation**

- Analyze customer demographics and psychographics
- Calculate customer acquisition cost if data available
- Determine customer lifetime value if possible
- Assess customer loyalty and retention metrics
- Evaluate the size and quality of customer database
- Identify customer concentration risks

### **3.3 Competitive Position Assessment**

- Identify unique selling propositions (USPs)
- List competitive advantages and their sustainability
- Identify main competitors and competitive threats
- Assess barriers to entry for new competitors
- Evaluate market share and position
- Analyze pricing power and strategy

### **3.4 Human Resources Evaluation**

- List all key employees and their roles
- Identify critical personnel whose departure would impact operations
- Assess the organizational structure efficiency
- Calculate total payroll as percentage of revenue
- Evaluate training requirements for new owner
- Identify any employment contract obligations

------

## **SECTION 4: RISK ASSESSMENT INSTRUCTIONS**

### **4.1 Key Person Dependencies**

- Identify all owner-specific relationships (suppliers, customers, employees)
- Assess transferability of these relationships
- Evaluate impact if key relationships don't transfer
- Determine owner's post-sale involvement requirements
- Identify technical skills or knowledge only the owner possesses

### **4.2 Market and Industry Risks**

- Analyze industry growth trends and projections
- Identify regulatory risks or upcoming changes
- Assess economic sensitivity of the business
- Evaluate technological disruption potential
- Identify demographic or social trends affecting the business
- Analyze geographic or location-specific risks

### **4.3 Operational Risks**

- Identify single points of failure in operations
- Assess supplier concentration and dependency
- Evaluate inventory obsolescence risks (if applicable)
- Analyze equipment age and replacement needs
- Identify lease terms and renewal risks
- Assess insurance coverage adequacy

### **4.4 Financial Risks**

- Evaluate accounts receivable quality and collection risks
- Assess working capital adequacy
- Identify any off-balance-sheet liabilities
- Analyze cash flow seasonality and management
- Evaluate contract terms with major customers
- Assess pricing sustainability

------

## **SECTION 5: GROWTH OPPORTUNITY INSTRUCTIONS**

### **5.1 Immediate Opportunities**

- List all quick-win improvements mentioned or identified
- Estimate revenue impact of each opportunity
- Calculate investment required for each opportunity
- Prioritize opportunities by ROI and ease of implementation
- Identify any low-hanging fruit in operations or marketing

### **5.2 Strategic Growth Potential**

- Assess market expansion possibilities (geographic, demographic)
- Evaluate product or service line extension opportunities
- Identify potential for operational improvements and cost savings
- Analyze digital transformation opportunities
- Evaluate potential for price optimization
- Assess acquisition or partnership opportunities

### **5.3 Growth Investment Requirements**

- Calculate capital needed for each growth initiative
- Determine timeline for each growth opportunity
- Assess risk level of each growth strategy
- Estimate realistic success probability
- Calculate expected ROI for major growth investments

------

## **SECTION 6: DEAL STRUCTURE ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS**

### **6.1 Purchase Terms Evaluation**

- Analyze the proposed payment structure
- Evaluate any seller financing terms (interest rate, duration, conditions)
- Assess earnout provisions if applicable
- Review escrow or holdback arrangements
- Identify any contingencies in the purchase agreement
- Evaluate non-compete and transition agreement terms

### **6.2 Working Capital and Inventory**

- Determine if working capital is included in purchase price
- Calculate normal working capital requirements
- Assess inventory valuation method and reasonableness
- Identify any working capital adjustments needed
- Evaluate accounts receivable and payable transfer terms

### **6.3 Asset and Liability Transfer**

- List all assets included and excluded from sale
- Identify any liabilities being assumed
- Evaluate lease assignment terms and conditions
- Assess contract and agreement transferability
- Review warranty or service obligations being assumed

------

## **SECTION 7: COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS**

### **7.1 Industry Benchmarking**

- Compare gross margins to industry averages
- Benchmark SDE margins against similar businesses
- Compare growth rates to industry standards
- Evaluate operational metrics against benchmarks
- Assess valuation multiple versus industry norms

### **7.2 Alternative Investment Comparison**

- Compare ROI to other investment opportunities
- Assess risk-adjusted returns versus alternatives
- Evaluate effort and time commitment versus other options
- Compare liquidity and exit options
- Analyze diversification benefits or concentration risks

------

## **SECTION 8: SCENARIO MODELING INSTRUCTIONS**

### **8.1 Financial Projections**

Create three detailed scenarios:

**Best Case Scenario:**

- Assume successful implementation of all growth opportunities
- Project 15-20% annual growth
- Include operational improvements impact
- Calculate 5-year cash flows and ROI

**Base Case Scenario:**

- Assume maintenance of current performance
- Project 3-5% annual growth (inflation)
- Include normal market conditions
- Calculate 5-year cash flows and ROI

**Worst Case Scenario:**

- Assume 20-30% revenue decline
- Include economic downturn impact
- Factor in loss of key customers or employees
- Calculate break-even point and survival requirements

### **8.2 Sensitivity Analysis**

- Identify the 3-5 most critical variables affecting profitability
- Calculate impact of 10%, 20%, 30% changes in each variable
- Determine which factors have the highest impact on returns
- Assess probability of negative scenarios

------

## **SECTION 9: DUE DILIGENCE PRIORITIES INSTRUCTIONS**

### **9.1 Red Flag Identification**

- List any inconsistencies in the provided information
- Identify unusual patterns or outliers in financial data
- Note any missing critical information
- Highlight any claims that seem too good to be true
- Flag any legal or regulatory concerns

### **9.2 Verification Priorities**

- Rank the top 10 items requiring independent verification
- Identify documents needed for verification
- List third-party confirmations required
- Prioritize based on impact on valuation and risk

### **9.3 Information Gaps**

- List all critical information not provided
- Identify additional data needed for complete analysis
- Prioritize information requests by importance
- Note any information that would change the valuation significantly

------

## **SECTION 10: QUESTION GENERATION INSTRUCTIONS**

### **10.1 Financial Questions**

Generate 10-15 specific questions about:

- Historical performance anomalies
- Add-back justifications
- Revenue recognition methods
- Customer and vendor terms
- Working capital requirements
- Capital expenditure needs

### **10.2 Operational Questions**

Generate 10-15 specific questions about:

- Daily operational procedures
- Key supplier and customer relationships
- Employee capabilities and retention
- Systems and processes documentation
- Competitive advantages sustainability
- Regulatory compliance status

### **10.3 Strategic Questions**

Generate 10-15 specific questions about:

- Seller's true motivation for selling
- Attempted growth initiatives and results
- Known challenges and obstacles
- Market changes and adaptations
- Future investment requirements
- Transition and training plans

### **10.4 Risk-Related Questions**

Generate 10-15 specific questions about:

- Potential liabilities and contingencies
- Insurance claims history
- Customer satisfaction and complaints
- Employee turnover and issues
- Legal disputes or threatened litigation
- Regulatory changes impact

------

## **SECTION 11: FINAL EVALUATION INSTRUCTIONS**

### **11.1 Strengths and Weaknesses Summary**

- List the top 5 strengths of the business
- List the top 5 weaknesses or concerns
- Assess which factors are fixable versus structural
- Evaluate the balance of strengths versus weaknesses

### **11.2 Valuation Conclusion**

- State whether the asking price appears fair, overpriced, or underpriced
- Provide a recommended offer range based on analysis
- Suggest optimal deal structure and terms
- Include conditions or contingencies to recommend

### **11.3 Go/No-Go Recommendation**

- Provide a clear recommendation on whether to proceed
- List conditions that must be met to proceed
- Identify deal-breakers that would warrant walking away
- Suggest negotiation priorities and strategies

### **11.4 Post-Acquisition Priority Plan**

If recommending to proceed:

- List immediate actions for first 30 days
- Identify 90-day priorities
- Outline first-year strategic initiatives
- Highlight critical success factors

------

## **OUTPUT FORMATTING INSTRUCTIONS**

1. **Use clear hierarchical headings** for each section and subsection
2. **Provide specific numerical analysis** wherever possible (percentages, ratios, dollar amounts)
3. **Include brief explanations** for all calculations and assessments
4. **Highlight critical findings** using bold or emphasis
5. **Create summary bullets** for key points in each section
6. **Use tables** for comparative data when appropriate
7. **Maintain professional, objective tone** throughout
8. **Provide confidence levels** for projections and estimates (High/Medium/Low)
9. **Include caveats and assumptions** for all analysis
10. **End each major section with key takeaways**

------

## **ANALYSIS EXECUTION NOTE**

Proceed through each section systematically. Do not skip sections even if data is limited - instead, note what information is missing and how it impacts the analysis. Be thorough but concise, focusing on actionable insights rather than generic observations. All analysis should be specific to the business being evaluated, not general business advice.
